BUSINESS INSIGHTS

1. The United Kingdom contributes the highest share of revenue and represents the largest customer base.

2. Total revenue generated during the analysis period was approximately £8.28 million.

3. Customer retention declines significantly after the first month, with most cohorts retaining only 20–40% of customers in subsequent months.

4. The December 2010 cohort demonstrated the strongest long-term retention performance.

5. Customer churn increases over time, indicating the need for loyalty and retention programs.

6. Cohort analysis revealed that a large percentage of customers do not make repeat purchases after their initial transactions.

7. Retaining existing customers can significantly improve long-term revenue compared to acquiring new customers.

8. RFM analysis can be used to identify high-value customers, loyal customers, and at-risk customers for targeted marketing campaigns.

9. Personalized promotions and loyalty rewards may help improve customer retention rates.

10. Data-driven customer segmentation can support more effective business decision-making and revenue growth.
